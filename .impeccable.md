# .impeccable.md

## Design Context

### Users
Study groups and solo learners using a shared Pomodoro timer in real-time. Public tool — anyone can join a room, typically students or remote workers who want the accountability and ambient social energy of studying together online. Context of use: long sessions (1–3 hours), browser open in the background or as the main focus of a second monitor.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: aesthetic, modern, clean

The interface should feel like stepping into a well-produced YouTube lo-fi study stream — calm, focused, slightly cinematic. Not cozy-cute, not corporate-sharp. More like a beautiful study environment that happens to be an app. Confidence without flash. Stillness without being boring.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Primary reference**: YouTube Pomodoro / lo-fi study videos. Think: dark backgrounds, large elegant timer typography, minimal controls, muted sophisticated palette, strong sense of atmosphere. The timer is the hero — everything else serves it.

**Theme**: Dark mode first. Deep, slightly warm neutrals (not cold tech-blue dark). The kind of dark that feels like a study room at night, not a developer terminal.

**Typography**: The timer display is the single most important element — it must be large, beautiful, and have clear personality. Use an elegant display or tabular font. UI text should be restrained and refined, not playful.

**Color**: Muted, sophisticated. One warm accent (not tomato-red — too aggressive for a calm focus tool). Think warm amber, soft gold, or a desaturated warm hue. Background layers should have subtle depth without glassmorphism gimmicks.

**Anti-references**: Avoid anything that looks like a generic productivity SaaS (bright blue CTAs, white backgrounds, card grids). Avoid lo-fi cliché (pixel art, anime rain gifs). Avoid AI slop palette (neon on dark, cyan/purple gradients).

### Design Principles
1. **Timer is everything** — the time readout is the hero of every screen. All layout decisions must serve it. Nothing should compete visually with the timer.
2. **Atmosphere over feature-density** — the interface should feel like an environment to inhabit, not a dashboard to manage. Reduce visual noise ruthlessly.
3. **Restrained sophistication** — no decorative elements without purpose. Every visual choice should feel considered, not filler. Dark, refined, typographically led.
4. **Calm hierarchy** — information reveals itself in layers. The most important thing (timer) is largest. Secondary things (mode, next step) are clearly subordinate. Tertiary (chat, music, participants) are available but never demanding attention.
5. **Social without distraction** — multi-room features (chat, music, participants) feel like ambient presence, not notifications screaming for attention.
